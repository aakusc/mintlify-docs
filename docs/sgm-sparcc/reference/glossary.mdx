# Glossary

This glossary defines key terms used throughout SGM and sales compensation governance.

## A

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Accelerator">
    A multiplier applied to commission rates when a salesperson exceeds their quota. For example, a 1.5x accelerator means commissions above quota are paid at 150% of the base rate.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Attainment">
    The percentage of quota achieved by a salesperson. Calculated as (Actual Results / Quota) Ã— 100. Also called "quota attainment" or "achievement."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="At-Risk Compensation">
    The portion of total compensation that depends on performance. Typically includes commissions, bonuses, and incentives. Contrasts with "base salary."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## B

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Base Salary">
    Fixed compensation paid regardless of performance. Part of Total Target Compensation (TTC) along with variable pay.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Booking">
    The recording of a sale in the compensation system. Bookings trigger commission calculations and crediting.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## C

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Clawback">
    Recovery of previously paid commissions when certain conditions occur, such as deal cancellation, customer churn, or fraud discovery. See SCP-001.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Commission">
    Variable compensation paid based on sales performance. Typically calculated as a percentage of revenue or a fixed amount per deal.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="CRB (Compensation Review Board)">
    Committee responsible for individual case decisions, exception approvals, and windfall deal reviews. Meets bi-weekly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Crediting">
    The assignment of sales credit to salespeople. Rules define who receives credit, how much, and when. See SCP-007.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## D

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Decelerator">
    A reduced commission rate applied below a certain attainment threshold. For example, paying 50% of the normal rate for the first 50% of quota.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Draw">
    An advance payment against future commissions. Can be recoverable (must be repaid) or non-recoverable (guaranteed minimum). See SCP-008.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## E

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Earnings">
    Commissions and bonuses earned but not necessarily paid. May be subject to holds, clawbacks, or other adjustments before payment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Exception">
    A deviation from standard policy requiring approval. Handled through the exception request process. See SCP-015.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## F

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Floor">
    A minimum payment guarantee regardless of performance. May apply during ramp periods or for specific situations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Framework">
    The overall governance structure including pillars, policies, committees, and processes. See Governance Framework Reference.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## G

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Gap Analysis">
    The process of comparing compensation documents against policy requirements to identify missing or incomplete coverage.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Guarantee">
    A minimum compensation level provided for a specific period, typically during onboarding or territory transitions. See SCP-008.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## H

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Hold">
    A temporary suspension of commission payment pending verification, approval, or investigation. Held amounts are released or forfeited based on outcome.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## I

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="ICM (Incentive Compensation Management)">
    Software and processes for managing sales compensation including plan design, calculations, payments, and reporting.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## K

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Kicker">
    A bonus payment for achieving specific objectives beyond standard quota, such as selling strategic products or acquiring new logos.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## M

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="MBO (Management by Objectives)">
    Bonus compensation tied to achieving specific business objectives rather than revenue targets.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Mid-Period Change">
    A modification to compensation plans during the performance period. Subject to transition rules. See SCP-010.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## O

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="On-Target Earnings (OTE)">
    Total expected compensation when 100% of quota is achieved. Includes base salary plus expected variable pay at target.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Overlay">
    A sales role that supports multiple territories or products and receives credit alongside primary sellers. Subject to split crediting rules.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## P

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pay Mix">
    The ratio of base salary to variable compensation. For example, a 60/40 pay mix means 60% base and 40% at-risk.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Policy">
    A formal document defining governance rules for a specific area of compensation management. See Policy Library Reference.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Q

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Quota">
    The target amount a salesperson is expected to achieve in a given period. Used to measure attainment and calculate commission rates. See SCP-002.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## R

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Ramp">
    A graduated schedule for new hires where quota and/or commission rates increase over time until reaching full productivity. See SCP-016.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rate Table">
    A schedule defining commission rates at different attainment levels, including accelerators and decelerators.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recoverable Draw">
    A draw that must be repaid from future commissions. Creates a negative balance that must be offset by earnings.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## S

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="SCP (Sales Compensation Policy)">
    Standard governance policies in SGM. There are 17 SCPs covering all aspects of compensation governance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Section 409A">
    IRS regulation governing deferred compensation. Non-compliance can result in significant penalties. See SCP-005.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="SGCC (Sales Governance Compliance Committee)">
    Primary governance committee for policy approval and compliance oversight. Meets monthly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="SPIF (Sales Performance Incentive Fund)">
    Short-term bonus programs for specific objectives. Subject to governance rules. See SCP-004.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Split Credit">
    When multiple salespeople receive credit for the same deal. Rules define how splits are calculated.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## T

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Territory">
    A defined set of accounts, geography, or other criteria assigned to a salesperson. See SCP-014.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="True-Up">
    An adjustment to reconcile estimated payments with actual results. Typically occurs at period end.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="TTC (Total Target Compensation)">
    The total expected compensation at 100% attainment. Same as OTE (On-Target Earnings).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## V

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Variable Compensation">
    Pay that changes based on performance. Includes commissions, bonuses, and incentives. Contrasts with fixed base salary.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## W

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Windfall">
    An unusually large deal that could result in disproportionate compensation. Subject to special review. See SCP-003.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## SGM-Specific Terms

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="AskSGM">
    AI-powered governance assistant that answers questions using policy knowledge base.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Binding Mode">
    Data source configuration: synthetic (demo), mapped (external APIs), or live (database).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Coverage Grade">
    Gap analysis rating (A, B, C) indicating how well a document addresses policy requirements.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Design Mode">
    SGM operational mode for creating policies, templates, and governance frameworks.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Dispute Mode">
    SGM operational mode for handling cases, exceptions, and disputes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="GovLens">
    AI-powered governance analysis engine that detects gaps and generates recommendations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Liability Score">
    Risk rating (1-5) from gap analysis indicating potential compliance exposure.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Operate Mode">
    SGM operational mode for daily operations, documents, and approvals.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Oversee Mode">
    SGM operational mode for executive oversight, committees, and compliance monitoring.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Patch">
    Pre-written remediation language to address a specific gap in a compensation document.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Tenant">
    A client organization in SGM with isolated data and configuration.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
